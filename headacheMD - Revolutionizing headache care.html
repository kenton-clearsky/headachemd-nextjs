Welcome to

HeadacheMD is a network of medical practices that employ an innovative and effective method for the diagnosis and treatment of headaches and neck pain due to nerve compression. Headaches caused by nerve compression can present as chronic migraine, chronic tension type headaches, or cervicogenic headache; targeted medical treatments are often helpful, and for those patients who do not experience reduction, nerve decompression surgery may be necessary.

Dr. Blake, along with collaborators at Harvard and also plastic surgeon Dr. Carlton Perry, produced scientific research proving the existence of abnormalities on the outside of the head in people with occipital nerve compression. These published studies have advanced the field of headache medicine significantly.

Dr. Blake has cared for patients with nerve compression headaches for over 20 years, and during that time she has optimized the medical management of nerve compression headaches. She has also worked closely with plastic surgeons to provide care for those patients whose pain requires nerve decompression surgery. Additionally, Dr. Blake explores and emphasizes the role of emotional factors as a contributing factor to pain, in order to identify and address those factors to maximize headache reduction. Through the combination of optimal medical and surgery treatment, along with management of other factors that can aggravate pain, sustained reduction in headaches can be achieved. Schedule a consultation to learn if you're a candidate for this innovative treatment approach.

Dr. Blake personally trains all Headache Medicine physicians located at headacheMD medical practices in the diagnosis and treatment, both non-surgical and surgical, of nerve compression headache. This ensures consistent, high-quality care across all locations.

The goal of the network is to reach more refractory headache patients globally in order to identify headaches due to nerve compression, and, if indicated, to consider the option of surgery, either in Houston or with a local, experienced surgeon. Complex and Fragile The creation story

This original painting by Isabelle Dupuy portrays structures of the head that are anatomically connected and relevant to headache. The brain and the meninges (the white layer surrounding the brain) respond to emotional and other stimuli that can create pain. The green occipital nerves (which are on the outside of the skull) can also become inflamed and irritated, leading to pain. Learn about emotional factors in headache treatment.
